Identifying Poor Signal Quality Symptoms

How can you determine if your signal quality is low? There are some indicators to watch for.

The sound from the person on the other end is not audible.

If you're unable to hear the person on the other end, chances are high that your signal quality is low. It can be challenging to have a conversation due to poor signal quality, causing disruptions.

There's a lot of static.

If you notice a lot of static on the phone, it's another sign that your signal quality is poor. Static can be annoying and make it difficult to carry on a conversation. If you find a lot of static on your line, your signal quality is likely poor.

The connection keeps dropping.

Frequent disconnections can indicate poor signal quality during phone calls. This can be particularly aggravating if you are having an important conversation. If your phone calls get dropped frequently, your signal quality is likely poor.

The sound is distorted.

If the sound on your phone is distorted, it's another sign that your signal quality is poor. This can make understanding the person on the other end difficult and complicate a conversation. What to Look for in Mobile Signal Boosters

To improve the quality of your mobile signal, you should consider searching for a mobile signal booster. When shopping for one, keep the following important things in mind.

Coverage area

The coverage area is the first thing you want to look for in a mobile signal booster. Most boosters will list the maximum coverage area on the box. Remember that this is usually the "theoretical" maximum coverage area, and your actual coverage may be less depending on factors like terrain, obstructions, and interference.

Gain of the booster

Another important factor to consider is the gain of the booster. The gain is a measure of how much the booster amplifies the signal. The higher the gain, the better. However, remember that too much gain can be bad, as it can cause the booster to amplify background noise along with the signal, which can degrade call quality.

The booster supports your carrier networks.

You also want to ensure your booster supports all the frequency bands your carrier uses. If you're in the UK, you've got several major carrier networks to choose from. But no matter your network of choice, ensuring that the signal booster you select supports all the frequency bands it uses is vital. To determine these frequencies and ensure your chosen booster will give you the boost you need, browse your carrier's website or call them.

External antenna

It's important to consider if the booster you're choosing has an external antenna. While many boosters have internal antennas suitable for most users, a booster equipped with an external antenna may offer better performance if you live in an area with bad cell reception.
